The HCI Standard

Five dimensions.
One score.

The Human Calibration Index scores content across the same five dimensions used internally by IN-KluSo's editorial team. No dimension is optional.

EVID
Evidence Quality
Source reliability, primary vs. secondary sourcing, verification depth. Can the claims be independently confirmed?
MECH
Mechanism Clarity
Causal chain completeness. Does the content explain why, not just what? Is the logic traceable and evidenced?
INC
Inclusivity of Perspective
Range of affected stakeholders represented. Are relevant voices present, or does the piece speak from a single vantage?
RISK
Risk Disclosure
Transparency about what the analysis cannot confirm. Are limits named? Is uncertainty treated honestly?
SPEC
Specificity
Precision of claims. No vague qualifiers where numbers, names, or dates are available. Territory and time window present.

Certification Levels

Four tiers.
One honest scale.

HCI scores produce a certification level. The level is shown on the badge. There is no minimum to submit — but only content scoring above 0.60 receives a certification badge.

Level 01
Reviewed
0.40–0.59
Scored and returned with detailed dimension feedback. No badge issued. Resubmit after revisions.
Level 02
Certified
0.60–0.74
Badge issued. Uncertainty noted on certificate. Meets the baseline human content standard.
Level 03
Verified
0.75–0.89
Full badge with Verified designation. Strong sourcing, clear mechanism, honest uncertainty handling.
Level 04
Distinguished
0.90–1.00
Distinguished badge. Anchor-quality content. Eligible for editorial feature in IN-KluSo archive.
